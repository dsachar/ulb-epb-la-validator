# ULB-EPB International Mobility Rules & Guidelines

*This document summarizes the official academic rules, regulations, and guidelines for incoming students at the Brussels School of Engineering (EPB) of the Université libre de Bruxelles (ULB), extracted from the official guidelines doc (v9, 02/07/2024).*

---

## 📋 Overview of Mobility Programs
The Brussels School of Engineering offers three main types of international mobility:
1. **Exchange Programmes (Non-graduating)**: e.g., Erasmus+ SMS. 1 or 2 terms stay. Credits are transferred back to the home institution; no ULB degree is issued.
2. **Double Degree Programmes (Graduating)**: Graduating path where the student receives a degree from both the home institution and ULB.
3. **Internships (Traineeships)**: Research period in an EPB laboratory. Non-graduating.

---

## 🎓 1. Non-Graduating Exchange Programs (Erasmus & Bilateral)

### Eligibility & Languages
- **Bachelor courses**: Requires French B2 level (strongly recommended).
- **Master courses**: Requires English B2 level (strongly recommended).
- *No official language certificates are mandatory, but B2 proficiency is highly recommended.*

### Learning Agreement (LA / OLA) Rules
Incoming exchange students must structure their Learning Agreement in accordance with the following 8 regulations:

*   **Rule 1 (ECTS Limits)**: 
    *   Minimum of **21 ECTS** credits per semester.
    *   Exactly **30 ECTS** is highly recommended for a single-term mobility.
*   **Rule 2 (60% Course Origin)**: 
    *   At least **60% of all credits** must be selected from the official EPB programs listed below.
    *   *Note: Mandatory course listings in the web catalog do not apply to exchange students.*
    *   **Eligible Master Programs (120 ECTS)**:
        - Electromechanical Engineering (`MA-IREM`)
        - Computer Science and Engineering (Professional) (`MA-IRIF`)
        - Architecture and Engineering (`MA-IRAR`)
        - Biomedical Engineering (`MA-IRCB`)
        - Civil Engineering (`MA-IRCN`)
        - Chemical and Materials Engineering (`MA-IRMA`)
        - Physical Engineering (`MA-IRPH`)
        - Electrical Engineering (`MA-IREL`)
    *   **Eligible Master Programs (60 ECTS)**:
        - Nuclear Engineering (`MS-NUAP`)
        - Heritage, Conservation and Restoration (`MS-GEPC`)
        - Nanotechnology (`MS-NATE`)
        - Transition Urbanism and Regional Planning (`MS-URDE`)
        - Data Science, Big Data (`MS-BGDA`)
    *   **Eligible Bachelor Programs (180 ECTS)**:
        - Bachelor in Engineering Sciences (`BA-IRCI`)
        - Bachelor in Engineering: Architecture (`BA-IRAR`)
*   **Rule 3 (Prior Permission)**:
    *   Selecting courses from the **60-credit Masters** (e.g. Data Science, Nuclear Engineering) requires prior approval from the academic coordinators.
    *   Selecting any course outside of the Rule 2 list (e.g. from the Faculty of Sciences or Bio-engineering) is permitted only with the **explicit written permission** of the professor in charge.
*   **Rule 4 (Majority Rule)**: 
    *   For Master-level exchange students, a **majority of the courses** (more than 50% of the credits) must be selected within one of the eight core 120-credit engineering Master programs.
*   **Rule 5 (Shared Courses)**: 
    *   If a course is co-organized/shared by EPB and another faculty, the ECTS credit value defined in the **EPB catalog** applies.
*   **Rule 6 (No Internships)**: 
    *   Exchange students are **not allowed** to enroll in internship courses `STAG-H501` and `STAG-H502`.
*   **Rule 7 (Project Constraints)**: 
    *   `PROJ-H417` (Development Cooperation Project) is **not allowed**.
    *   `PROJ-H418` is open only if the student has French **C1 proficiency**, stays for the **full academic year**, and is selected via motivation review.
*   **Rule 8 (Master Thesis)**:
    *   **SMS (Study Exchange)**: Master thesis is supervised and defended at ULB. Students must secure a supervisor agreement and a provisional title from an EPB advisor before applying.
    *   **SMP (Internship/Traineeship)**: Thesis is prepared at ULB under a co-supervisor but defended at the home institution (credits set by the home institution).

## 🏆 2. Double Degree (DD) Programs

### Eligibility & Requirements
- Only students from partner universities with active Double Degree agreements are eligible.
- **Language**: English B2 minimum for Master courses.
- **Academic Standard**: Student must have an ECTS grade of at least **"D"** (top 90% of passing students) in prior studies.

### Course Selection Guidelines
Double Degree students are generally assimilated to native ULB students:
- **Rule 1 (Standard Curriculum)**: Must comply fully with the signed DD agreement and follow standard ULB program structures.
- **Rule 2 (Core Modules)**: Only **120-credit Masters** are eligible (Electromechanical `MA-IREM`, Computer Science `MA-IRIF`, Architecture `MA-IRAR`, Biomedical `MA-IRCB`, Civil `MA-IRCN`, Chemical `MA-IRMA`, Physical `MA-IRPH`, Electrical `MA-IREL`). Students must take the program's mandatory courses. Course equivalences for courses already passed at the home university require approval from the ULB master coordinator.
- **Rule 3 (Exclusions)**: Courses from **60-credit Masters** (Data Science `MS-BGDA`, Nuclear `MS-NUAP`, Nanotech `MS-NATE`, Transition Urbanism `MS-URDE`, Heritage Conservation `MS-GEPC`) or other faculties are **not eligible**, unless explicit permission is granted by the EPB mobility coordinator.
- **Additional DD Constraints**:
  - French language courses **cannot** count towards the graduation credit total.
  - Yearly programs exceeding **60 ECTS** require signing a declaration of risk acknowledgment.

## 💼 3. Incoming Internships & Traineeships

- **Payment**: Incoming interns are legally **forbidden** from receiving any Belgian salary or grant.
- **Erasmus+ SMP (Traineeships)**:
  - Must identify an EPB hosting professor who initiates the administrative process.
  - Requires nomination by Lola Veyrat **4 weeks prior to arrival**.
- **Non-European Placements**:
  - Stays under an institutional bilateral agreement: visa for study (max 12 months, no work permit needed).
  - Stays without an agreement: stays > 3 months require a work permit (not managed by ULB).
